# Midnight Sakura Neko — Lore Bible

## Core Premise
In **Hanamichi City**, every spring night feels one step removed from reality. Neon signs glow through drifting sakura petals, old train lines cut through moonlit districts, and people quietly chase second chances.

At the center is **Yuna**, an anime catgirl who appears whenever someone is emotionally lost. Some call her an urban legend. Others swear she saved them from becoming strangers to themselves.

The story asks:
**Can two people who wear masks in daylight recognize each other at midnight?**

---

## Setting: Hanamichi City
A fictional Japanese-inspired megacity blending classic neighborhoods and modern transit life.

### Districts
- **Shiun Station Ward**: final-train atmosphere, vending machine glow, chance encounters.
- **Sakura Alley**: hidden pedestrian lanes lined with old cherry trees and lanterns.
- **Clocktower Hill**: historical district with a sakura clocktower tied to local myths.
- **Kohaku Riverside**: quiet dawn promenade where confessions often happen.

### Seasonal Rule
Sakura season lasts only a short window each year. During bloom nights:
- people report strange coincidences,
- old memories feel sharper,
- unresolved relationships resurface.

The city itself never becomes magical-fantasy; the "magic" is emotional timing and place.

---

## The Midnight Myth
Locals call it **"The Neko Hour"**:
- Between the last train and first light,
- under active sakura bloom,
- people at emotional crossroads may meet a cat-eared girl.

Rumors say:
1. She appears only when someone is avoiding a truth.
2. She always offers a path, never an answer.
3. If you ignore her twice, the city goes quiet around you.

Whether this is supernatural or social myth is left intentionally ambiguous.

---

## Tone & Genre
- **Genre**: romantic urban drama with light mystery
- **Tone**: soft, intimate, bittersweet, hopeful
- **Pacing**: quiet conversations, meaningful choices, emotional momentum

No world-ending stakes. Stakes are personal:
- trust,
- vulnerability,
- choosing presence over performance.

---

## Themes
1. **Masks vs authenticity** — who are you when no one is watching?
2. **Timing** — late is not always too late.
3. **Distance** — emotional avoidance as self-protection.
4. **Small courage** — tiny honest choices reshape futures.

---

## Route Pillars
- **Warm Route (Romance-first)**: emotional openness, shared nights, direct vulnerability.
- **Cold Route (Mystery-first)**: guarded dialogue, rumor investigation, slow trust.
- **True Route (Integration)**: emotional honesty + myth resolution.

---

## Canon Rules
- No hard fantasy powers on-screen (no spells, no combat magic).
- The world remains physically grounded.
- Yuna is real as a character; the myth around her stays interpretable.
- Endings depend on repeated choice patterns, not single "gotcha" picks.
